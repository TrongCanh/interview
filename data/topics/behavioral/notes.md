## ü§ù L√†m vi·ªác nh√≥m / Teamwork

### 2. Conflict Resolution

**Example Answer (STAR):**

**Situation:** "In my previous project, I was working with a senior developer who insisted on using a technology stack I wasn't familiar with, which was causing delays."

**Task:** "I needed to complete my tasks on time while maintaining good working relationship."

**Action:** "I scheduled a one-on-one meeting to understand his perspective. I explained my concerns about the timeline and proposed a compromise: we would use his preferred stack for the core features, and I would document everything for future reference. I also spent extra time learning the technology."

**Result:** "We completed the project on time, and I gained valuable skills. The developer appreciated my proactive approach, and we continued to work well together on future projects."

**Key Points:**

- Focus on resolution, not blame
- Show empathy
- Demonstrate problem-solving

### 3. Collaboration

**Example Answer:**

**Situation:** "Our team was building a new feature with tight deadline, involving frontend, backend, and DevOps."

**Task:** "We needed to coordinate effectively to deliver on time."

**Action:** "I proposed daily stand-ups and set up a shared documentation space. I took ownership of the API design and worked closely with the backend team. When the frontend team had issues, I helped debug and provided clear documentation."

**Result:** "We delivered the feature 2 days ahead of schedule. The team praised the clear communication, and our documentation became a template for future projects."

### 4. Dealing with Difficult Team Members

**Key Strategies:**

- Stay professional
- Focus on work, not personality
- Document interactions if necessary
- Seek mediation if needed
- Find common ground

## üí™ K·ªπ nƒÉng gi·∫£i quy·∫øt v·∫•n ƒë·ªÅ / Problem Solving

### 5. Challenging Situation

**Example Answer:**

**Situation:** "Our production database went down during peak traffic hours, affecting thousands of users."

**Task:** "Restore service quickly and prevent future occurrences."

**Action:** "I immediately alerted the team and started investigating. I identified the issue as a memory leak in a recent deployment. I rolled back the deployment while the team worked on a fix. After restoring service, I led a post-mortem to identify root causes and implemented monitoring to catch similar issues early."

**Result:** "Service was restored within 30 minutes. We implemented better monitoring and testing procedures, and similar incidents decreased by 80%."

### 6. Mistakes and Failures

**Example Answer:**

**Situation:** "I once pushed a bug to production that caused payment failures for some users."

**Task:** "Fix the issue immediately and prevent recurrence."

**Action:** "I immediately acknowledged the mistake, rolled back the deployment, and worked with the team to fix the bug. I personally reached out to affected users to apologize and ensure their issues were resolved. I also implemented additional testing and code review processes."

**Result:** "All affected users were compensated, and trust was restored. The new testing process caught similar issues in the future, and I earned respect from my team for taking ownership."

**Key Points:**

- Take responsibility
- Focus on learning
- Show how you improved

### 7. Tight Deadlines

**Strategies:**

- Prioritize tasks (Eisenhower Matrix)
- Communicate early if deadline is unrealistic
- Break down large tasks
- Focus on MVP first
- Ask for help if needed

## üöÄ Leadership & Initiative

### 8. Leadership Experience

**Example Answer:**

**Situation:** "Our team lacked a clear process for code reviews, leading to inconsistent quality."

**Task:** "Establish a code review process and get team buy-in."

**Action:** "I researched best practices and proposed a review checklist. I volunteered to review all code for two weeks to demonstrate the process. I also created documentation and conducted a workshop to train the team."

**Result:** "Code quality improved significantly, with 60% fewer bugs in production. The team adopted the process company-wide, and I was promoted to tech lead."

### 9. Taking Initiative

**Example Answer:**

**Situation:** "I noticed our team was spending hours on repetitive manual tasks."

**Task:** "Automate the tasks to save time."

**Action:** "I learned scripting in my free time and built automation tools. I tested them thoroughly and presented the solution to my manager. After approval, I trained the team on using the tools."

**Result:** "We saved approximately 10 hours per week, which the team could use for more valuable work. The automation became part of our standard workflow."

### 10. Mentoring Others

**Key Approaches:**

- Lead by example
- Provide constructive feedback
- Share knowledge proactively
- Be patient and supportive
- Celebrate others' successes

## üîÑ Th√≠ch nghi & H·ªçc h·ªèi / Adaptability & Learning

### 11. Learning New Technologies

**My Learning Process:**

1. **Research**: Read documentation and tutorials
2. **Practice**: Build small projects
3. **Apply**: Use in real projects
4. **Share**: Teach others to reinforce learning

**Example Answer:**

**Situation:** "Our team decided to migrate from jQuery to React."

**Task:** "Learn React and lead the migration."

**Action:** "I took an online course, built several practice projects, and started refactoring small components. I created a style guide and conducted workshops to help the team. I also set up a code review process to ensure best practices."

**Result:** "The migration was completed in 3 months with minimal disruption. Team productivity increased by 40% due to better component reusability."

### 12. Adapting to Change

**Example Answer:**

**Situation:** "Our company underwent a major reorganization, changing our team structure and processes."

**Task:** "Adapt to new structure and maintain productivity."

**Action:** "I embraced the change by learning about the new processes and building relationships with new team members. I volunteered to help with the transition and provided feedback to management."

**Result:** "Our team was one of the first to adapt and maintain productivity. I was recognized for my positive attitude and was asked to help with future transitions."

### 13. Handling Feedback

**Example Answer:**

**Situation:** "My manager gave me feedback that my code reviews were too critical and demotivating."

**Task:** "Improve my feedback style while maintaining code quality."

**Action:** "I asked for specific examples and advice. I started using the 'sandwich method' (positive, constructive, positive) and focused on the code, not the person. I also asked for feedback on my feedback."

**Result:** "Team members reported feeling more supported, and code quality remained high. My manager noted significant improvement in my communication style."
